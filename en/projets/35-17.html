The story

A shared horizon.

On the seventeenth floor, Paris doesn't grow distant, it draws nearer. The roofs gain depth, the Seine curves, the Eiffel Tower becomes an intimate landmark.

A living area running east to west opens onto the kitchen and looks directly onto the panorama. On the night side, four bedrooms all face the same direction: two master suites with their dressing rooms, and two further bedrooms, one of which has its own bathroom.

The furniture stays in its place: signature, lasting, chosen so as not to compete with what takes place outside. At dusk, the room fades; what remains is the light of Paris.
