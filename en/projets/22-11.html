The story

Breaking the loop.

The apartment turned in on itself. On the courtyard side, the living area. On the Eiffel Tower side, three bedrooms in enfilade: a 19th-century circulation, where one slept on the noble side. Our take: break this loop.

The living area has shifted skyward. The kitchen, now open, extends the room toward the unobstructed panorama of the Eiffel Tower. The three bedrooms have moved to the quiet of the courtyard; the main one has its own bathroom and dressing room.

At every hour of the day, Paris draws itself into the living-room windows. Nights have gained silence, days have gained light. The fittings, sober and precise, accompany this self-evidence without ever commenting on it.
